Understanding Web Accessibility

Web accessibility ensures that all people, regardless of their abilities or disabilities, can access and use the internet. It's about creating websites that are usable by everyone, including those with visual, motor, auditory, speech, or cognitive disabilities.

Our Approach to Accessibility

At OsloMet, we adhere to the WCAG 2.1 guidelines to ensure our website is accessible. We regularly use tools like selinium extenstion or webdriver to test our site.

Implementing Accessibility in Code

We use semantic HTML to structure our website and ensure screen readers can navigate our content effectively. Our CSS is designed to provide sufficient color contrast and responsive layouts for all devices.

Going Beyond Automated Testing

Automated tests don't catch everything. That's why we manually check our site to ensure it's fully navigable via keyboard and that all interactive elements are accessible.
